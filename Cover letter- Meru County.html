Real-World Scenario: Tea Farmer in Meru South

Mama Wanjiku grows tea in Abothuguchi. She feels severe stomach pain but can't afford to miss work during harvesting season. Through her chama leader, she gets an Ayuka Yamo code (subsidized by Meru County). She fills symptoms on her phone while taking tea break. Within 4 hours, three Meru-based medical professionals (including a doctor in Meru town and a nurse in Timau) validate her diagnosis: early-stage ulcer, treatable at home with medication. She visits nearest dispensary with diagnosis report. Gets treatment immediately. Total cost: KSh 500 diagnosis + KSh 800 medication = KSh 1,300. Days missed: Zero. Alternative: Travel to Meru town (KSh 600 transport), wait 6 hours at hospital (KSh 1,500 lost income), doctor consultation (KSh 1,000), same medication (KSh 800) = KSh 3,900 + full day lost.
